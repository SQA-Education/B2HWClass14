Interviewing for a Job • Strengths, Weaknesses, and Interview Answers

This homework only practices Class 14 content: strengths and weaknesses vocabulary, common interview questions, and explaining how you handle stress or challenges.

1. Reading

Answering Interview Questions

Lina is preparing for a job interview. She is confident about talking about her strengths, such as being collaborative, detail-oriented, and patient. She also knows how to talk about a weakness honestly without sounding negative.

For example, Lina says that she used to feel insecure when speaking in front of large groups, but she has improved by practicing presentations at work. When interviewers ask how she handles stress, she explains that she makes a task list, prioritizes urgent work, and communicates clearly with her team.

She knows employers want honest, thoughtful answers with examples, not perfect answers without details.

2. Vocabulary

Match the strength or weakness word to its meaning.

Real-life English tip: In interviews, a weakness answer sounds stronger when you explain how you are improving it.
