Interviewing for a Job • Strengths, Weaknesses, and Interview Answers

This homework only practices Class 14 content: talking about strengths and weaknesses, giving honest examples, and answering common interview questions about yourself.

1. Reading

Answering Interview Questions

Lina is preparing for a job interview. She is confident about talking about her strengths, such as being collaborative, detail-oriented, and patient. She also knows how to talk about a weakness honestly without sounding negative.

For example, Lina says that she used to feel insecure when speaking in front of large groups, but she has improved by practicing presentations at work. When interviewers ask how she handles stress, she explains that she makes a task list, prioritizes urgent work, and communicates clearly with her team.

She knows employers want honest, thoughtful answers with examples, not perfect answers without details.

2. Vocabulary

Match the strengths and weaknesses word to its meaning.

Real-life English tip: When talking about a weakness, follow the pattern weakness → example → what you’re doing to improve. This sounds honest without sounding negative.

3. Writing

Write 6–8 sentences answering this interview question: “What are your strengths and weaknesses?” Give a real example and say how you are improving the weakness.

Words: 0

Grammar tip: Use used to for past habits you have changed (I used to feel nervous…), the present perfect for progress (I have improved…), and the present simple for what you do now (I organize my tasks…).

4. Speaking

Speak for 30–45 seconds answering: “What is one strength and one weakness you have?” Give an example and say how you are improving.
